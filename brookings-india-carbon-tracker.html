FAQs and Instructions

About the tracker

What is this? The Brookings India Carbon Tracker is a near-real-time electricity generation tracker that shows electricity generated by source (eg Renewable, Thermal) at a national level. In addition to this, it also shows total CO2 emitted every 5 minutes and CO2 emissions per kWh of electricity generated. It also displays the moving averages of each generation source, as well as some summary statistics.

Why is this necessary? Hitherto, national electricity generation data was only available on a daily basis at best. A tracker like this makes it possible to draw patterns and inferences about electricity generation in the country for planning and policy purposes. For example, it helps us see the cleanest time of day for charging electric vehicles, or the value and impact of adding additional thermal generation capacity.

Who are we? Brookings India is India's leading policy research think tank. We are an independent arm of the Washington, DC based Brookings Institution. You can view some of our other work at www.brookings.in

Instructions

How do I use this? If you are visiting from a computer, you will be shown the current week's generation data by default
If you are visiting from a mobile or tablet, you will be shown the summary statistics page.
You can switch between different views and dates with the selectors at the top of this page.
The Corrected Generation Data and Raw Generation Data views show electricity generation by source at a 5-minute granularity, as well as CO2 emissions and emissions intensity. On these pages, you can view and hide data on the charts by clicking their corresponding entries in the legend.
The Moving Average views show you the daily, weekly and monthly moving averages of each generation source. Similar to the Corrected and Raw Generation Data pages, you can view and hide data by clicking on entries in the legend.
On all these views, you can also zoom, pan, and scale the data.
Finally, the Summary Statistics page shows some interesting statistics for the period you have selected, such as total tons of CO2 emitted, and the average daily peak generation in MW.

Glossary Demand Met - Total National Electricity Demand
Net Demand - Total Generation Minus Renewable Generation
